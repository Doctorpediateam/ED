ED Do’s and Don’t’s

If you’re looking to avoid erectile dysfunction as much as you can, here are some tips on what to do (and NOT do).

Diet

Basically, if it’s bad for your heart, then it’s bad for your love life.  If food/drink can interrupt or restrict blood flow in your arteries, then it can do exactly the same thing to blood flow to the penis. Your diet should include plenty of fruits and vegetables because an excessive amount of fatty, processed and fried foods decrease circulation. Did you know that some studies have shown that men who consume a Mediterranean diet (fruit, vegetables, whole grains, nuts, fish, red wine) are least likely to experience erectile dysfunction?

Maintenance

Once you’ve gotten to a good weight, you need to maintain it. If you're overweight, it can lead to Type 2 Diabetes which causes nerve damage all over the body. If the nerves that are in contact with the penis are damaged, it can lead to erectile dysfunction.

High Blood Pressure/High Cholesterol

Both of these conditions can inflict damage on blood vessels – which include those that supply blood to the penis. Keep a check on both your blood pressure and cholesterol levels and do your best to keep them low by diet, exercise, and seeing a doctor for medication.

Alcohol

If you can’t drink in moderation, then don’t drink at all. Chronic drinking causes liver and nerve damage which ultimately affect your ability to keep an erection.

Exercise

Aerobic exercise – such as running or swimming – is great for helping prevent erectile dysfunction. Be careful that your exercise of choice doesn’t put too much pressure on the perineum (the area between your anus and scrotum) such as bike riding. This pressure can affect the blood vessels and nerves that supply the penis. If you’re going to bike a lot, doctors recommend you wear padded cycling pants and stand up when you can while pedaling.

Testosterone

After you turn 40, testosterone levels fall approximately 1.3% a year - and they fall sharply around the age of 50. If you’re exhibiting a lowered sex drive, moodiness or having difficulty making decisions then you may have low testosterone – so check with a doctor.

Anabolic Steroids

These are used and abused by some athletes and bodybuilders, and they can wreak havoc on your body by shrinking the testicles and interfering with their ability to produce testosterone.

Stop Smoking

Cigarette smoking can also harm the blood vessels flowing to the penis – the nicotine causes them to contract.
