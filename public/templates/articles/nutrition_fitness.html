Erectile Dysfunction - Nutrition & Fitness

ED is caused by poor blood vessel function and is considered a sign you could be a candidate for heart disease. Enter: diet and exercise. The same things that help your heart could also help you not become impotent. Studies show a combination of diet and exercise can improve the risk of getting ED or impotence for millions of men.

In one study, a low-fat, low-cholesterol diet along with exercise resulted in about a third of the men with ED returning to healthy sexual functioning. In another study, men with a diet high in flavonoids had a 10 percent less risk of developing ED. Flavonoids are food compounds found in fruits, vegetables, and grains. Dietitians say the Mediterranean Diet is high in flavonoids.

What Other Foods are Helpful?

Believe it or not, garlic. It contains the enzyme allicin which helps improve blood flow. Soybeans are rich in L-Arginine, an amino acid which boosts circulation. Soy is also good for your prostate. You've heard that oysters are an aphrodisiac. It turns out they are high in zinc. Zinc deficiency can lead to erectile problems. Omega 3 fatty acids improve the nervous system and circulation. You want to make sure your blood is flowing to all the right places. Oily fish like mackerel, sardines, and salmon are all high in Omega 3.

Two dietary supplements have shown promise in treating ED in clinical trials: L-arginine and ginseng. Researchers say they appear to work by allowing penile relaxation and engorgement. But those supplements should only be used under a doctor's supervision since they may interfere with other medications.

Helpful Exercises for Erectile Dysfunction

Viagra doesn't have to be your only option to treat ED. A British study found that pelvic exercises helped 40 percent of men with ED regain normal erectile function. Another 33.5 percent of the men in that study had significantly improved function with pelvic exercises.

You've heard of Kegel exercises for women.  Well, they can benefit men too by strengthening the bulbocavernosus muscle. This muscle has three jobs. It lets blood flow to the penis so it can engorge during an erection. It pumps during ejaculation, and it helps empty the urethra after urination.

To find the muscles you need to do the Kegel exercises, stop your stream several times in the middle of urination. Feel the clenching? Those are the muscles. Another way is to squeeze your buttocks like you are holding a bowel movement. Hold for five to 10 seconds while breathing, then relax all your muscles.

One more note.  Besides Kegels, add aerobic exercise to your routine. It will help you ward off obesity, diabetes, high cholesterol, and vascular disease. All of those can affect blood flow and result in ED.

So the good news is that a healthy diet, along with regular exercise keeps your weight down, keeps your heart-healthy and may be the key to good sexual health.
