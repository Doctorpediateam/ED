Erectile Dysfunction Available Treatments

The good news is that there are a variety of treatments for erectile dysfunction – but be aware that not all will work in chronic cases. All treatments come with their own pros and cons and it’s best you discuss with your primary physician to see what’s best for you.

First Step

Before you can treat erectile dysfunction, you must first learn what your specific cause(s) is/are. Treatments can range from surgical to medication.

Non-Surgical

Psychological

Talking to a trained therapist about your issues might be all you need if, indeed, your cause is anxiousness, poor communication with a lover, or something that resides in the mind.

Medications

We’ve all heard of Viagra (AKA Sildenafil), Levitra (AKA Vardenafil), Stendra (Avanafil) and Cialis (AKA Tadalafil) and that’s because it many cases they work.

So how do they work? They constrain Phosphodiesterase V (PDE V), an enzyme that can take away an erection. By briefly blocking PDE V, Nitric Oxide (NO) can get to work, allowing you to achieve an erection. Before that can happen, however, you need both stimulation and foreplay.

In some cases, men find that the prescribed amount of Viagra isn’t enough and give up on it.

After what you feel is an adequate trial, talk to your doctor. There are dosage adjustments and other medications that can be tried. Do not give up! Sometimes simply changing a medication you will see better results.

You could ask your physician if increasing dosage might help you see better results. Cialis doesn’t require an empty stomach to take effect and some patients prefer it for that reason alone.

There is a serious drug interaction between ED medications and the therapeutic classification of nitrates. Tell your doctor ALL the medications, vitamins, over-the-counter drugs, and herbal products you may be taking.

If you’re on any medication that contains nitrates, use caution. The combination can lead to hypotension (low blood pressure). Side effects commonly associated with these medications include upset stomachs, nasal congestion, headaches and even brief disturbances in vision.

Warning: Many websites sell medications that purport to help with erectile dysfunction and so we advise you use extreme caution dealing with them. The FDA warns that illegal sites may give you medications with no known origin, low quality, or that are even the wrong drug. Remember: all treatment should be done with the advice of a doctor – even “natural” or “herbal” supplements.

Also be careful when dealing with dietary supplements. Herbal pills have not been proven to have any effect and in certain cases, the FDA warns of harm or risks for pills sold on the Internet.

Hormones

Consult with your doctor to see if this treatment is best for you. It’s easy to replace hormones with non-invasive practices like topical gels, creams and even patches. Injections and pellets are the next step up but be sure to get your Doctor’s approval.

Mechanical Aids

Vacuum devices as well as penile constriction rings fall under this category and might aid with erections. The vacuum is a cylinder that, once placed over and around the penis, is then evacuates the air inside. The remaining vacuum allows blood to flow into the penis – and the ensuing erection is maintained by transferring the band from the bottom of the cylinder onto the base of the penis for up to half an hour. The main con here seems to be the vexation on not having the spontaneity to perform at will and the burden of the device.

 

Penile Injection Therapy

Otherwise known as intracavernosal injection therapy, this treatment requires you to make your own injections into the erection chambers of the penis itself. The needle/syringe itself is tiny and only a small amount is required. The three medicines most commonly associated with the treatment are alprostadil, papaverine and phentolamine. Side effects might include pain or penile scarring – or even a painful erection lasting longer than a couple of hours.

Surgical Treatments

A variety of options focusing on penile prostheses are available. Simple outpatient surgery is required to insert inflatable cylinders in the penis’s erection chambers and a pump is added in the scrotal sac. By pressing the pump, fluid is transferred into the cylinders making the penis erect.

Be aware that completely eliminating ED caused by a medical condition (ex: high blood pressure) via diet/exercise might not be possible.
