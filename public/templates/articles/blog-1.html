ED Facts & Tips

Avoid consumption of alcohol. If you are addicted to alcohol, then restrict the intake. Alcohol acts on central nervous system and causes erectile dysfunction.2

Erection medications simply boost blood flow into the penis. They have no effect on arousal, which is subjective. That's one reason why more than half of men who get an initial prescription for an erectile dysfunction drug never refill it.3

When it comes to boosting sexual performance, many men will walk all over the Earth looking for ways to maintain a good sex life. Luckily men, all you have to do is walk – not run – 2 miles a day. This, along with other healthier lifestyle interventions can help obese men reduce their risk of ED, or even “reverse” current impotence, according to a 2005 study.5

It is normal that men will lose their erections about 25% of the time.1

If you have trouble getting or maintaining an erection less than one in five times, it is probably nothing to get too upset about.4 

Practice yoga and meditation regularly to keep away anxiety, depression, and stress. Meditation and yoga are best stress busters.2

If you can get and maintain an erection 75% of the time or more, then chances are your erectile issues are situational, not biological.1

Marijuana, heroin, cocaine, etc. cause erectile dysfunction. Withdrawing from addiction to the above-mentioned substances really helps in increasing erections and sexual stamina.2 

Another factor to consider with Erectile Dysfunction is, are you waking up with erections in the morning?  If the answer is yes, then chances are your ED is situational.1

If you are masturbating to very stimulating porn on a regular basis, then it might be difficult to get as aroused in the bedroom with your partner.1

Another factor that contributes to ED is not having enough stimulation before beginning or attempting sex.  Many men have a sexual script that is kissing for a minute or two and then trying to proceed to intercourse. 1

In a 2005 study, three months of twice-daily sets of kegel exercises combined with biofeedback and advice on lifestyle changes, such as quitting smoking, losing weight, and limiting alcohol, worked far better than just giving the participants advice.5

Traditionally, doctors concerned about blood flow during sexual intercourse have recommended a sex position with the woman on top. However, actual studies that measured blood flow and heart rate during intercourse did not find this position to be any better than others.4

You can also consider using a penis ring if you can get an erection but have trouble maintaining it.  They work by trapping the blood inside your penis, so it remains harder for longer.  You can purchase one in a sex store or online for about $5 and you put in on over your penis and testicles when you are half hard and keep it on until you are finished with sex.  It’s a great cheap fix for erectile issues.1
