ED Tips & FAQs

Details

Here are some more random tips and do’s, don’ts, and everything-in-betweens concerning erectile dysfunction.

Erectile dysfunction affects approximately 15 to 30 million men in the United States.1

In many cases, however, erectile dysfunction is very much the result of dietary patterns and can be an early warning signal of more serious health risks such as heart attack or stroke, which are also diet- and lifestyle-related.2

The penis is a vascular organ, so it needs good blood flow to work well. Lack of blood flow over time can cause damage that can lead to erectile dysfunction. Diabetes, which is closely associated with being overweight or obese, can also lead to nerve damage in the penis.1

One reason blood flow can be impaired is the buildup of plaque in the blood vessels that stiffens their normally flexible walls and prevents them from dilating in response to sexual arousal (in other words, when your arteries harden, your penis won’t). Plaque buildup results from chronically high cholesterol levels, and a subsequent inflammatory response from the body’s white blood cells.2

Even if you're already a healthy weight, exercise can further help enhance your sexual performance. Several studies have shown that, even among healthy men, those who exercise have less risk of developing erectile dysfunction compared to those who are sedentary. Exercise also tends to improve libido and your overall mood, both of which can contribute to a satisfying sex life.1

Obviously, diet is also linked to maintaining a healthy weight. Specifically, men who are worried about erectile dysfunction should focus on a diet that's good for their heart because the circulatory system is a big part of erectile functioning. That means eating lots of fruits and vegetables while avoiding a too much fat, animal protein, salt, and sugar is the smart choice.1

For 25 percent of men with erectile dysfunction, medication can be ineffective—since their difficulties are largely psychological. To avoid performance anxiety, men and their partners should reframe their ideas of good sex.5

Drinking warm water mixed with honey and lemon is very good for health. It helps in losing weight, and for this simple reason, people who want to lose weight should take it for sure.3

Experts still aren't exactly sure why they're associated, but just being sleep deprived can have a very negative effect on erections (beyond simply being too tired for sex).1

Just as stress can affect someone at any age, it can cause erection problems in the young as well as the old. It may even be part of the reason why greater numbers of younger men are complaining of erectile issues. Although many young men turn to drugs to address erectile dysfunction, meditation, yoga, and massage can also be used to treat it because erectile dysfunction is more likely to have a psychological, rather than physical, basis when it occurs in younger men.1

Chinese herbalists consider carrots a good remedy for treating ED due to their high beta-carotene content.4

Alcohol in moderation shouldn't do any permanent damage. Alcohol abuse, however, can lead to liver problems and nerve damage, which can also cause erectile dysfunction.1
