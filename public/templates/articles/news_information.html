Erectile Dysfunction and Testosterone Replacement

Research findings reported in the Journal of Urology show long-term replacement treatment with testosterone perks up sex life in men with ED. On self-assessments surveys, the patients placed high marks for their quality of life and erectile and sexual health. Investigators from the School of Medicine at Boston University and Public Health (BUSPH) teamed with German urologists and testosterone deficient males between 50 and 60 years of age.  The  results showed both sexual and urinary systems’ functioning improved while on testosterone replacement therapy.

Viagra: No Link to Melanoma Skin Cancer and Erectile Dysfunction Drug

The Journal of the National Cancer Institute states that the skin cancer, melanoma is not related to the erectile dysfunction drug Viagra. Viagra and additional drugs in the erectile dysfunction therapeutic classification are on a side effect watch list by the US Food and Drug Administration. The trends showed an 11 percent increase of skin cancer melanoma cases in users of the drugs. However, researchers were not able to establish a cause and effect.

Rather the trend was given to ED patients increased knowledge of their overall health.

They were more likely to visit a healthcare provider increasing the chances for detection and diagnosis.

Blockbuster Competitive Erectile Dysfunction Drug Global Market Massive

A comprehensive review, in collaboration with industry experts, shows the erectile dysfunction medication area exceeding 2.95 billion USD by the 2023 year end.  The movers include the growing numbers of men experiencing impotence, aging population, an increasing number of ED drugs on the market and in the pipeline, and expirations of drug patents allowing for an explosion of generics medications.  Currently, Cialis, Levitra, Stendra, Viagra, and Zydena are approved drugs for ED. Growth in regional countries include Americas, Europe, Middle East, Asia-Pacific, and Africa. The prime market share is in North America, followed by Europe.

Additional factors projected for competition and growth are pre-existing pricing structure, insurance coverage as a lifestyle drug, and market population size.  Many interested companies include Apricus Biosciences Inc., Bayer AG., Eli Lilly and Company, Meda Pharmaceuticals Inc., Pfizer Inc., and Vivus Inc. In the US, ED industry revenues exceed 1.65 billion dollars annually.

Erectile Dysfunction Tied to Parkinson’s disease as Predictive Indicator

Looking at study statistics the information shows that men with ED appear to have a 52% higher risk of acquiring Parkinson’s later in life. Head investigator, Tengu Hsieh, MD looked at the information from the Taiwan National Health Insurance database. The comparison of 3,253 men with ED and 12,162 men without ED showed a higher occurrence of Parkinson’s over time.

Reported in the Journal of Clinical Neurology, lower testosterone level may be the first sign of the early indications of Parkinson’s movement symptoms. A follow-up study published in the American Journal of Epidemiology cited similar results.  The central nervous system, motor coordination, and movement are affected in Parkinson's disease.

Erectile Dysfunction and Oral Health Gum Disease

Investigators looking into a possible link between chronic periodontal disease and erectile dysfunction found five published studies. The information from over 200,000 individuals from 20 to 80 years of age. ED was 2.28 times more common in men with periodontitis as compared to the controls. Periodontitis is an inflammation of the gums due primarily to dental plaque and gingival irritation. One study demonstrated a link with ED. There was sexual improvement after successful treatment of periodontal disease. The drawbacks is that it did not take into account that both conditions, ED and gum disease, have the same risk factors and causes, such as smoking, age, cardiovascular disease, and diabetes. Therefore, the results may be inconsistent and questionable at this time.

Erectile Dysfunction: Exploration of Low-intensity Shockwave Therapy (LIST)

Futuristic shockwave treatment is a growing market in ED therapy. It is for men who have not found anything else that works. For example, the popular medications from the phosphodiesterase-5 inhibitors (PDE5i) classification, such as Viagra.  The new technology uses non-harmful shock waves to enhance blood flow to the penis. Also, it appears to accelerate tissue regeneration to grow new blood vessels. Alert! Larger clinic research studies are needed to demonstrate reproducible results.

Announcing: Erectile Dysfunction Topical Gel

The topical gel for impotence is a valuable treatment option for some men. However, the downside is the product produces discomfort in male users. While, this feeling goes away within 5 to 20 minutes after application, 20% of men reported achy soreness from the medication.

The study described no other adverse reactions. The product is applied directly to the penis and it contains, alprostadil, which absorbs through the skin.  Approximately, 67 to 75% of men were able to generate and maintain an erection versus only 17% of men achieved an erection with a placebo.
