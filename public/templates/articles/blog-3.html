The Good & Bad About Natural ED Treatments

There are more holistic approaches to curing erectile dysfunction out there that are worth covering. Here are some natural treatments for ED with their pros and cons. As always, talk with your doctor before starting any type of treatment (even natural ones).

DHEA

DHEA – also known as dehydroepiandrosterone – is produced by adrenal glands so, as such, is a natural hormone. It can then be converted to either estrogen or testosterone, and the dietary supplement made from it also contains wild yam as well as soy.

Studies show that men suffering from erectile dysfunction are more likely to also suffer from low levels of DHEA. In fact, a study in 2009 revealed men receiving DHEA are more able to achieve/maintain an erection. DHEA is also a good treatment for erectile dysfunction in men with diabetes.

Rhodiola Rosea

This herb seems to help with energy and reduces fatigue – as well as increase sexual ability. One study showed that men taking 150-200mg a day for three months experienced improved function in the bedroom.

Panax Ginseng

Known as the herbal version of Viagra, Panax (also known as red ginseng) is backed by good research. Seven recent studies of men dosed with anywhere from 600mg-1000mg three times a day found evidence of its effectiveness against erectile dysfunction.

Scientists suggest the reason for its effectiveness is its anti-inflammatory action and ability to improve blood flow. It seems to work best on men with high lipids and metabolic syndrome.

L-arginine

This is an amino acid that’s always naturally present inside your body helping to create nitric oxide. That nitric oxide helps relax blood vessels allowing for a good erection.

Studies are positive too; 31% of men who suffer erectile dysfunction took 5 grams of L-arginine a day and found their function greatly improved. In fact, another study showed that by combining pycnogenol (a plant product taken from the bark of a tree) with L-arginine it takes only two months to restore the sexual function of almost 80% of participants. Within three months, 92% of participants had their sexual ability restored. It’s recommended for mild-to-moderate erectile dysfunction.

Acupuncture

Acupuncture has been a part of us for centuries, but studies are still mixed. Having said that, many studies show positive results when it’s used to treat erectile dysfunction. A study in 1999 discovered that acupuncture worked in improving erectile function in around 39% of the participants and a study in 2003 took that number to 21%.  If you’re going to try this procedure, make sure you go to a licensed acupuncturist.

Yohimbe

This is a supplement that’s taken from the bark of the Yohimbe tree (found in Africa). Studies have shown the use of Yohimbe has a positive effect on sexual ability. Unfortunately, because there’s no clear evidence of its good effects and its side effects include elevated blood pressure and heart rate, the American Urological Association does not recommend it. Talk to your doctor before using it.
